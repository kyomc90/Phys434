As we can see from the plot above, if the mean for different Gaussian distributions, the sum of these distributions will still be Gaussian distribution, but when the mean is different for two different distributions, their range are different, so their sum cannot sum up to a Gaussian distribution. It is the same for average of different distributions. And also, central circuit theorem stated that most distributions when summed and averaged over enough trials will converge to a Gaussian distribution.
